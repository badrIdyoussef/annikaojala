About Me
My story

Her unique story as an advocate for justice and equality has garnered interest from media and online audiences alike, positioning her as an inspiring voice in the global conversation around human rights, representation and wellbeing.

A seasoned advocate and strategist for change, Annika coordinates international partnership initiatives and human rights campaigns in the European Parliament.

Prior to working in EU politics, Annika co-created highly successful electoral campaigns in Finland, which led to historic advancements of women in leadership positions, notably in the Sanna Marin government.

Annika’s dedication to inclusive leadership and human rights extends beyond her political work. She has collaborated with organizations such as the OSCE and Google, developing solutions for inclusive and safer communities online and offline.

Her strategic approach and experience in navigating complex political landscapes make her uniquely positioned to drive positive change and foster collaborations across sectors.

Her theory of change is rooted in green sustainable action, interlinking progress on human rights to the creation of ecologically sustainable communities. In 2019 she initiated the Feminist Climate Ambassadors programme, and the international Feminist Climate Conference.

Born in Helsinki, Annika now lives in Brussels after years lived in Johannesburg and Paris.

Leadership roles

Annika has held various leadership roles, serving as board member in gender equality and LGBTIQ+ rights organizations.

These include among others the Finnish National Committee for UN Women and the Coalition of Women’s Associations and the Finnish National Committee for UN Women.

As the Chair of the International Affairs Committee for Seta – LGBTI Rights in Finland, Annika lead a team of experts assisting Finland’s leading LGBTI rights organization’s board of directors on global LGBTIQ+ rights. She represented the organization at various international conferences from Europe to Oceania.

She put forward intersectional trans-inclusive feminist policies as the national representative of Finland on the Women in Politics working group of the European Women’s Lobby, and served as vice chair of the board of directors for KIOS Foundation.

Education
Studied at

Annika studied political science and philosophy at Université Paris VIII and humanities at Humak University of Applied Sciences. She specialized in gender equality, diversity and inclusion in political parties.

She has earned certifications in :

  • Management at Harvard Business School Online (2021).
